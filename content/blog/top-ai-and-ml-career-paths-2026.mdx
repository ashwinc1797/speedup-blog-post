## What is AI and ML — Clear Explanation

Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) are two interconnected fields that have revolutionized the way we live and work. AI refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that would typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making. ML, on the other hand, is a subset of AI that involves the use of algorithms and statistical models to enable machines to learn from data and improve their performance over time. To illustrate this concept, consider a simple analogy: imagine a child learning to recognize different objects, such as dogs and cats. At first, the child may struggle to distinguish between the two, but as they see more examples and receive feedback, they begin to develop a mental model that allows them to accurately identify each object. Similarly, ML algorithms work by learning from large datasets and using this knowledge to make predictions or take actions. According to a report by Accenture Pune, the use of AI and ML can help businesses improve their efficiency by up to 40%, making it an essential technology for companies to adopt.

The concept of AI and ML can be further explained by considering the different types of machine learning: supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning. Supervised learning involves training a model on labeled data, where the correct output is already known. Unsupervised learning, on the other hand, involves training a model on unlabeled data, where the model must find patterns or structure in the data. Reinforcement learning involves training a model to take actions in an environment to maximize a reward. For example, a self-driving car may use reinforcement learning to learn how to navigate through a city, while a chatbot may use supervised learning to learn how to respond to customer queries.
