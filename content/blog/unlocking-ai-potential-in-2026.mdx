## What is AI 
Artificial intelligence refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making. In simple terms, AI is like a computer program that can think and act like a human being, but much faster and more accurately. For example, imagine a self-driving car that can navigate through the streets of Pune, avoiding traffic and pedestrians, without any human intervention. This is made possible by AI algorithms that can process vast amounts of data, recognize patterns, and make decisions in real-time. According to a report by McKinsey, AI has the potential to automate up to 30% of jobs in the next decade, which highlights the need for developers to acquire new skills and adapt to the changing job market.

As we explore the world of AI, it's essential to consider the various types of AI, including narrow or weak AI, general or strong AI, and superintelligence. Narrow AI refers to systems that are designed to perform a specific task, such as image recognition or language translation. General AI, on the other hand, refers to systems that can perform any intellectual task that a human can. Superintelligence refers to systems that are significantly more intelligent than the best human minds, which is still a topic of debate and research. In our experience, we've seen that most developers are interested in building narrow AI systems, which can be used to solve specific problems or improve existing processes.
